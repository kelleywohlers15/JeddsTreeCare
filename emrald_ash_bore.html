Emerald ash borer

When were they found

Emerald ash borer is an exotic beetle that was discovered in southeastern Michigan near Detroit in the summer of 2002.

Abou the Emrald Ash Borer

The adult beetles nibble on ash foliage but cause little damage.

The larvae feed on the inner bark of ash trees, disrupting the tree's ability to transport water and nutrients.

Where they can be found

As of October 2018, it is now found in 35 states, and the Canadian provinces of Ontario, Quebec, New Brunswick, Nova Scotia and Manitoba.
